TypeSentence 1Sentence 2
OpinionIt is often argued that [topic]...I completely agree/disagree that... because [কারণ ১] and [কারণ ২].

Body Paragraph — ৪টি বাক্য (Topic → Explain → Example → Link back)
Opinion — Agree/DisagreeAgree/Disagree
P1 Topic
Although [বিপরীত মত], it is clear that [তোমার মত].
Although some argue that university degrees guarantee success, it is clear that practical skills are far more valued by modern employers.
P1 Explain
This means that [result], as [reason].
This means that many talented graduates remain unemployed for years, as employers prioritise experience and problem-solving ability over academic certificates.
P1 Example
For example, [case], which clearly shows that [link].
For example, a 2022 OECD report found that 40% of graduates work in roles unrelated to their degree, which clearly shows that academic qualifications alone do not determine career outcomes.
P1 Link back
This highlights that [broader importance — নতুন angle].
This highlights that education systems must urgently adopt more diverse assessment methods to unlock every student's true potential.
P2 Topic
A particularly serious consequence of this is that [claim], which [result].
A particularly serious consequence of this is that students accumulate significant debt, which leaves them financially trapped at the very start of their adult lives.
P2 Explain
This is because [reason], which ultimately leads to [result].
This is because tuition fees continue to rise without guaranteed returns, which ultimately leads to graduates spending decades repaying loans that severely limit their financial freedom.
P2 Example
For instance, [case], which further demonstrates that [link].
For instance, research revealed that average graduate debt now exceeds $30,000, which further demonstrates that the financial burden of higher education outweighs its benefits for many students.
P2 Link back
This suggests that [implication or solution].
This suggests that governments must urgently reconsider how higher education is funded before an entire generation is priced out of opportunity.
Discussion — Both ViewsDiscussion
P1 Topic
While [view 1], it is evident that [view 2].
While many employees prefer remote work for its flexibility, it is evident that office environments foster the collaboration and creativity that drive genuine innovation.
P1 Explain
This means that face-to-face interaction allows teams to solve problems instantly, as spontaneous discussion produces solutions that no digital platform can fully replicate.
P1 Example
For example, a Harvard Business School study found that office-based teams generated 30% more innovative ideas, which clearly shows that physical proximity remains essential for creative collaboration.
P1 Link back
This highlights that organisations which abandon office culture entirely risk sacrificing the creative energy that gives them a long-term competitive advantage.
P2 Topic
Nevertheless, a particularly strong argument here is that [claim], which [result].
Nevertheless, a particularly strong argument here is that remote work eliminates exhausting daily commutes, which gives employees more time and energy to perform at their best.
P2 Explain
This is because hours previously spent travelling can be redirected toward focused work, which ultimately leads to higher productivity and significantly improved employee wellbeing.
P2 Example
For instance, a Stanford University survey found that remote workers reported a 20% increase in job satisfaction, which further demonstrates that flexibility is a powerful driver of long-term professional performance.
P2 Link back
This suggests that a hybrid model combining the structure of office work with the flexibility of remote arrangements would best serve both employers and employees.
Advantage/DisadvantageAdv/Disadv
P1 Topic
Although [concern], it is evident that [advantage].
Although social media raises serious concerns about privacy, it is evident that it has fundamentally transformed how communities connect and share knowledge globally.
P1 Explain
This means that individuals can maintain meaningful relationships across continents, as instant communication removes the geographical barriers that once made long-distance connection impossible.
P1 Example
For example, a Pew Research survey found that 75% of users feel more connected to distant family members through social media, which clearly shows that it strengthens rather than weakens human social bonds.
P1 Link back
This highlights that social media, when used responsibly, has the power to bring people together in ways that previous generations could never have imagined.
P2 Topic
A particularly serious drawback beyond this is that [claim], which [result].
A particularly serious drawback beyond this is that excessive social media use causes chronic anxiety among teenagers, which places enormous pressure on already overstretched mental health services.
P2 Explain
This is because constant exposure to idealised images creates deep feelings of inadequacy, which ultimately leads to depression and social withdrawal in young people still developing their sense of identity.
P2 Example
For instance, a 2023 Oxford University study found that teenagers spending over 4 hours daily on social media were 50% more likely to experience depression, which further demonstrates that unrestricted use seriously damages mental health.
P2 Link back
This suggests that governments and parents must work together to introduce clear screen time limits before the mental health consequences become irreversible.
Problem/SolutionProblem/Solution
P1 Topic
Although [popular belief], it is clear that [real problem].
Although individual recycling is widely promoted, it is clear that industrial emissions remain the primary driver of dangerous air pollution levels in most major cities.
P1 Explain
This means that personal lifestyle changes alone can never reverse industrial damage, as factories release carbon at a rate that individual action cannot meaningfully offset.
P1 Example
For example, a WHO report found that industrial activities contribute 65% of global air pollution, which clearly shows that corporate emissions must be the primary target of any serious environmental policy.
P1 Link back
This highlights that without holding corporations legally accountable for their emissions, no meaningful improvement in air quality can realistically be achieved.
P2 Topic
A particularly effective solution is to [action], which [result].
A particularly effective solution is to introduce strict carbon taxes on heavy industries, which financially compels corporations to invest in cleaner production methods.
P2 Explain
This is because financial penalties create a direct economic incentive for companies to adopt greener technologies, which ultimately leads to measurable reductions in harmful emissions and improved urban air quality.
P2 Example
For instance, Sweden introduced a carbon tax in 1991 and reduced industrial pollution by 30% within two decades, which further demonstrates that economic policy is one of the most powerful tools for environmental protection.
P2 Link back
This suggests that with the right financial incentives in place, governments can drive industrial change far more rapidly than awareness campaigns or voluntary commitments ever could.
Cause/EffectCause/Effect
P1 Topic
Although [common belief], research suggests that [real cause].
Although financial stress is commonly cited as the leading cause of rising divorce rates, research strongly suggests that unrealistic expectations shaped by social media play an equally destructive role.
P1 Explain
This means that couples compare their relationships to idealised online portrayals, as social media presents a distorted standard that no real relationship can consistently meet.
P1 Example
For example, a 2022 Cambridge University study found that couples who actively use social media are 40% more likely to report relationship dissatisfaction, which clearly shows that digital comparison is a significant driver of marital breakdown.
P1 Link back
This highlights that addressing the psychological impact of social media on relationship expectations is just as important as tackling the financial pressures couples face.
P2 Topic
A particularly worrying effect of this is also that [claim], which [result].
A particularly worrying effect of this is also that children from broken families struggle academically and emotionally, which creates cycles of instability extending into the next generation.
P2 Explain
This is because parental conflict creates a chronically unstable home environment, which ultimately leads to poor concentration, lower academic achievement and a higher likelihood of repeating dysfunctional relationship patterns in adulthood.
P2 Example
For instance, research from the University of Chicago revealed that children of divorced parents are 60% more likely to drop out of school, which further demonstrates that family instability has profound and lasting consequences for child development.
P2 Link back
This suggests that investment in family counselling and early emotional support for children of divorced parents is not a luxury but an urgent social necessity.
Conclusion — ১-২ বাক্য
TypeFormulaExample
OpinionTo conclude, I strongly agree/disagree that [মত] because [কারণ ১] and [কারণ ২].To conclude, I strongly disagree that university education should be free because it burdens taxpayers and devalues qualifications.
DiscussionTo conclude, while [view 1], [view 2]. In my opinion, [তোমার মত].To conclude, while remote work offers flexibility, offices foster creativity. In my opinion, a hybrid model would be most effective.
Adv/DisadvTo conclude, although [topic] offers [advantage], its [disadvantage] cannot be ignored.To conclude, although social media has revolutionised communication, its damaging effects on mental health cannot be ignored.
Problem/SolutionTo conclude, [problem] is caused by [cause]. However, [solution] can effectively address this.To conclude, air pollution is primarily caused by industrial emissions. However, carbon taxes can significantly reduce this problem.
Cause/EffectTo sum up, [topic] is caused by [cause 1] and [cause 2], which results in [effect 1] and [effect 2].To sum up, rising divorce rates are caused by financial stress and social media expectations, which results in emotional trauma for children and long-term family instability.
